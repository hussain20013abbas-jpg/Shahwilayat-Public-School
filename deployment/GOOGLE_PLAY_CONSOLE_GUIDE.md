# Google Play Console: Account Setup & Cost

## 1. Account Cost
*   **One-Time Fee:** $25 (USD).
*   **No Annual Fee:** Unlike Apple's App Store ($99/year), Google Play has a one-time registration fee.
*   **Payment Method:** A valid international credit or debit card is required.

## 2. Step-by-Step Account Creation
1.  **Google Account:** Log in to your Google Account (preferably a professional one for the school).
2.  **Go to Play Console:** Visit [play.google.com/console/signup](https://play.google.com/console/signup).
3.  **Choose Account Type:** Select **Organization** (since it's for a school).
4.  **Fill in Organization Details:**
    *   School Name: Shahwilayat Public School.
    *   Address: Official school address.
    *   Phone Number: School's official contact number.
    *   Website: [https://shahwilayat.edu.pk/](https://shahwilayat.edu.pk/)
5.  **Identity Verification:** You may need to provide a government-issued ID (like CNIC or Passport) to verify your identity.
6.  **Pay the Fee:** Enter your card details and pay the $25 fee.
7.  **Wait for Approval:** Google usually takes 2-7 days to verify your account.

## 3. Optimizing for Approval (Avoid Rejection)
Google Play has strict policies. Follow these tips:
*   **PII Handling:** Ensure your Privacy Policy clearly states how you handle student data (CNIC, names, photos).
*   **Broken Links:** Make sure all links (like "Join Meeting") are valid or have a fallback.
*   **Clear Purpose:** The app must provide value. Since it's a school management system, it's clearly functional.
*   **No Placeholder Content:** Remove any "Lorem Ipsum" or test data before submitting.
*   **Login Credentials:** Provide a test student and admin account for Google's reviewers to test the app.
*   **App Content Rating:** Be honest in the content rating questionnaire (e.g., "No ads," "No user-generated content").

## 4. Low-Cost Publishing Alternatives
*   **Progressive Web App (PWA):**
    *   **Cost:** $0.
    *   **How:** Add a `manifest.json` and a service worker to your React app.
    *   **Benefit:** Users can install it directly from Chrome/Safari without the Play Store.
*   **APK Sharing:**
    *   **Cost:** $0.
    *   **How:** Build an APK and share it via WhatsApp or the school's website.
    *   **Benefit:** Instant distribution, but users must manually enable "Unknown Sources."
*   **Amazon Appstore:**
    *   **Cost:** $0 (usually).
    *   **Benefit:** Another platform for Android users, though less popular than Google Play.
